Why Home Loan Percentage Rates Matter So Much?

Okay so basic thing first. When you take a home loan, you don’t just borrow money. You also pay interest on that money every month for years. That interest is what we call Home Loan Percentage Rate. Even a small difference in rate can make a huge difference in your EMI and total amount you end up paying.

For example, 8.2% vs 8.5% may look small but when loan is 30 lakh or 50 lakh, the amount becomes massive over 20 years. That’s why comparing Home Loan Percentage Rates is basically the most important part before signing anything.

Credit score (CIBIL score – if low, your rate go up)
Your income stability
Company type (government job people usually get lower rates)
Loan amount you want
Property location
Type of property (flat, house, plot, commercial etc.)

Fixed Rate vs Floating Rate – Confusion That Never Ends

Let's talk about this because every home buyer get confused here.

Fixed Rate Home Loan

Rate stays same from start to end
EMI stable
But sometimes higher than floating
Good if you want stability

Floating Rate Home Loan

Rate moves according to RBI repo rate and market
EMI may increase or decrease
Usually cheaper at start
Good for long-term borrowers expecting rate drop

VSR Fincare normally explains both options clearly so user can choose what suits their long-term plan. Many people just ask “ tell lowest home loan rate ” but choosing between fixed and floating also change cost.
