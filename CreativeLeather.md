When you start making jewelry, one of the small but important things people mostly ignore is cord thickness. It might look like a small detail, but trust me, it can change the whole look and comfort of your jewelry piece. Whether you are making a bracelet, necklace, or anklet, knowing how to choose the right cord thickness for jewelry can make your design more durable and stylish.
At Creative Leathers, we work with a wide range of cords — leather, suede, waxed cotton, braided, and more — and we’ve seen how cord size affects both the look and feel of handmade jewelry. So, let’s talk about how you can select the perfect cord thickness for your design, step by step.

1. Understand Why Cord Thickness Matters

Cord thickness is basically the width or diameter of your string or leather cord. It affects how your jewelry piece feels when worn and how it holds up over time.
Too thin cords can snap easily, especially if you add heavy pendants or beads.


Too thick cords might not fit through your beads or clasps and can make the jewelry feel bulky.


The right balance gives you both strength and comfort. That’s why jewelry makers always check the cord gauge before starting a project.

2. Consider the Type of Jewelry You’re Making

Each jewelry type needs a specific cord size. Here’s a simple way to think about it:
Bracelets: Usually between 1mm to 2mm works well. If you’re making a delicate wrap bracelet, go thinner (around 1mm). For a chunkier leather cuff, 2mm or more looks better.


Necklaces: Depends on design. 1.5mm to 3mm cords are popular. Thin cords look elegant for minimalist pendants, while thick ones add a bold, rustic style.


Anklets: Go for 1.5mm or 2mm – strong enough for daily wear but still flexible.


Keychains or charms: You can use 3mm or thicker cords since durability matters more than comfort here.


Choosing thickness also depends on the weight of beads and pendants you’re using. Heavier ones need thicker cords to avoid breaking.

3. Match Cord Thickness with Bead Hole Size

One of the most common beginner mistakes is ignoring bead hole size. If your cord doesn’t fit through the beads, you’ll waste time and material.
Here’s a small tip from the Creative Leathers workshop:
Always test your cord with the largest and smallest beads before cutting long pieces.
For example:
If you have beads with a 2mm hole, choose a 1mm or 1.5mm cord.


For larger 4mm holes, you can go up to 2mm or 3mm cord easily.


This balance keeps your design neat and functional.

4. Think About the Material of the Cord

Different materials behave differently, even if they are the same thickness. A 2mm leather cord might feel thicker than a 2mm cotton cord because of stiffness. Let’s look at a few popular options:
Leather cords: Natural and durable, great for boho and rustic jewelry. Creative Leathers offers smooth, braided, and flat leather cords in many thicknesses (from 1mm to 5mm).


Waxed cotton cords: Slightly flexible and good for casual designs. They slide through small bead holes easily.


Suede cords: Soft and flat, best for wrap bracelets and chokers.


Nylon cords: Very strong, good for macrame and knot-based jewelry.


Each cord material gives a different look, feel, and strength, so try matching it with your jewelry purpose.

5. Comfort and Flexibility

When people wear jewelry daily, comfort matters more than style sometimes. A thick cord may look bold but can feel stiff or heavy around the neck or wrist. For example:
For everyday necklaces, go for something light (1.5mm–2mm).


For fashion statement pieces, you can try thicker ones (3mm or braided).


If your design needs to move freely, like in knot jewelry or layered bracelets, thinner cords help with flexibility.

6. Durability and Purpose
Ask yourself — how long should the jewelry last?
 If you are making a temporary fashion piece, even a thin cord works fine. But if you’re creating something to wear every day, choose a sturdy cord with a minimum 2mm thickness or go for braided leather cords which are known for strength.
Creative Leathers’ braided leather cords, for example, not only look rich but also handle daily wear easily without cracking or stretching.

7. Color and Style Balance
Thickness also affects how color appears. On thick cords, colors look bolder and more visible; on thin cords, they appear soft and subtle.
Try matching your jewelry theme:
Thin natural brown cords for vintage or rustic looks.


Thick black cords for bold and masculine designs.


Bright colorful cords for boho and casual vibes.


Sometimes, a mix of different thicknesses in layered jewelry gives an artistic and handmade charm — a trend loved in 2025 jewelry designs.

8. Always Do a Small Test
Before finalizing your cord, make a small sample piece. Try adding beads, tying knots, or fixing clasps. See if the cord bends nicely or looks stiff. If it frays easily or loses shape, that’s a sign to pick a different thickness.
This small step saves both time and material — something every jewelry crafter learns the hard way!


10. Don’t Forget the Clasps and Findings
    
Clasps, jump rings, and crimp ends all depend on your cord size. A thick cord might not fit through a small clasp or metal end. Always check that your cord thickness matches your findings.
For example:
1mm cords fit well with small lobster clasps and knot ends.


2mm–3mm cords go well with barrel clasps or adjustable knots.


Flat cords need wide ribbon ends or crimps.


10. The Final Touch – Your Creative Style

In the end, the “right cord thickness” depends not just on measurements but also on your creative expression. There’s no strict rulebook — only experience and experimentation.
If you love minimalist jewelry, thinner cords with delicate charms will match your taste. If you prefer bold statement designs, go for thick braided leather or multi-strand cords.
